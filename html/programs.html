B.Tech (Aerospace Engineering)

Program Educational Objectives (PEOs)

  • PEO I: Demonstrate knowledge and reasoning skills in core areas of aerospace engineering with expertise in design, modelling, and analysis.
  • PEO II: Practice innovative professional skills to recognize problems, formulate, analyze, and propose optimal solutions for space systems.
  • PEO III: Exhibit proficiency in effective teamwork and attain strategic leadership positions in aerospace and allied domains.

Program Outcomes (POs)

  1. PO1: Engineering knowledge – Apply mathematics, science, engineering fundamentals, and specialization to solve complex engineering problems.
  2. PO2: Problem analysis – Identify, formulate, review literature, and analyze complex problems using mathematical, natural sciences, and engineering principles.
  3. PO3: Design/development of solutions – Design solutions for complex problems considering public health, safety, cultural, societal, and environmental considerations.
  4. PO4: Conduct investigations of complex problems – Use research methods, experiment design, data analysis, and synthesis to provide valid conclusions.
  5. PO5: Modern tool usage – Select and apply appropriate engineering and IT tools including modeling and prediction, understanding their limitations.
  6. PO6: The engineer and society – Apply reasoning informed by societal, health, safety, legal, and cultural knowledge relevant to engineering practice.
  7. PO7: Environment and sustainability – Understand societal and environmental impact of engineering solutions and demonstrate need for sustainable development.
  8. PO8: Ethics – Apply ethical principles and commit to professional ethics and responsibilities.
  9. PO9: Individual and team work – Function effectively as an individual and in diverse, multidisciplinary teams, including leadership roles.
  10. PO10: Communication – Communicate effectively on complex engineering activities through reports, presentations, and instructions.
  11. PO11: Project management and finance – Apply engineering and management principles to manage projects and multidisciplinary environments.
  12. PO12: Life-long learning – Recognize the need for and ability to engage in independent, life-long learning amid technological change.

Program Specific Outcomes (PSOs)

  • PSO 1: Apply aeronautical and space systems concepts to analyze and test aerodynamics, structural, and propulsion systems in professional practice.
  • PSO 2: Design and simulate interplanetary space missions, aircraft instrumentation, and test communication protocols of flight vehicles.
